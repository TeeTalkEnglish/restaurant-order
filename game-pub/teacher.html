How to Run This Lesson

Setup instructions before you begin

📱
Student device: Open game-pub/student.html — the student reads the pub menu as a real customer would. No tooltips — they must ask you!
👩‍💻
Teacher device: Keep this page open. Follow the script scene by scene. Use the challenges when the student is doing well!
🎭
Stay in character! Speak naturally as Aussie pub staff. Use real expressions: "no worries", "mate", "cheers", "how ya going?". Don't correct mid-sentence!
🍺
Pub culture tip: In Australia, pubs often have table service for food but you order drinks at the BAR. Explain this to the student — it's different from a café!
⏱️
Timing: This role-play should take 20–30 minutes. After each scene, pause briefly to give quick feedback.

Extra Challenge Scenarios

Use these if your student is doing well!

🍺 Wrong Drink Size

Give them the wrong size beer and see if they speak up!

"Here's your middy of VB!" (they ordered a schooner)

🍗 No More Parmas!

Tell them the Parma is sold out — most popular dish!

"Sorry mate — we actually just ran out of Parmas! Can I suggest the schnitzel instead?"

🔞 ID Check

Ask for ID — see if they respond calmly and naturally.

"Sorry mate, I just need to check your ID quickly — do you have your passport?"

💳 Card Surcharge

Mention the surcharge and see if they understand and accept.

"Just so you know, there's a 1.5% surcharge on Visa cards. Is that all good?"

🧾 Split the Bill

Ask if they want to split the bill with their workmates.

"Do you want to split this between the table or are you all paying separately?"

🍽️ Wrong Order

Bring the wrong meal — can they correct you politely?

"Here's your Beef Schnitzel!" (they ordered the Chicken Parma)

🎉 Recommend Special

Push tonight's special and see if they ask follow-up questions.

"Hey, just letting you know — tonight's special is a Parma plus a schooner for \$32. It's a ripper deal!"

🔇 Noisy Pub

Pretend you couldn't hear — they must repeat their order!

"Sorry mate, it's pretty loud in here! Could you say that again?"

Aussie Pub Vocab Cheat Sheet

Use these naturally in the role-play

Mate
= Friend. Aussies say this to everyone! "Thanks mate!"
No worries
= No problem. Use constantly at a pub!
How ya going?
= How are you? Very casual Aussie greeting.
Sweet as
= Great / Awesome. "Sweet as mate!"
Legend
= Amazing person. "You're a legend!"
Ripper
= Excellent / Great. "That's a ripper deal!"
Schooner
= Large beer glass (425ml). Most common size.
Pot / Middy
= Smaller beer glass (285ml).
All up
= Total amount. "That's \$37.50 all up."
Parma
= Chicken Parmigiana. Most iconic Aussie pub meal!
Schnit
= Schnitzel. Crumbed and fried meat cutlet.
Chips
= French fries. NOT potato chips (those are "crisps").
Mash
= Mashed potato. Common side dish alternative.
Happy hour
= Discounted drinks for a set time period.
RSA
= Responsible Service of Alcohol. Australian law!
EFTPOS
= Card payment machine. "Tap and go" / debit card.
Surcharge
= Extra fee for using a credit card. Usually 1–2%.
Cheers
= Thanks OR goodbye. Very common in pubs!
Counter meal
= A meal ordered at the bar counter of a pub.
Arvo
= Afternoon. "Friday arvo" = Friday afternoon.
Goes off
= Gets very busy / lively. "This place goes off!"
Tap beer
= Draft beer poured from a tap. Common in Aussie pubs.

Assessment Rubric

Use this to evaluate your student's performance

Skill Area What to Look For Level
🗣️ Greetings & Small Talk Uses casual greetings naturally; responds to "how ya going?" appropriately; makes small talk about the pub or the week ★★★★★
🍽️ Ordering Food Orders a full meal confidently; chooses sides and sauces; handles sold-out items gracefully ★★★★★
🍺 Ordering Drinks Knows beer sizes (schooner/pot); orders at the bar; understands happy hour ★★★★★
🔞 Handling ID Check Responds calmly and naturally; not offended; understands RSA policy ★★★★★
❌ Handling Problems Politely corrects wrong orders; handles sold-out dishes; deals with broken EFTPOS calmly ★★★★★
💳 Paying the Bill Asks for the bill; understands card surcharge; says goodbye warmly ★★★★★
🇦🇺 Aussie Vocabulary Uses or understands: mate, no worries, cheers, ripper, schooner, happy hour ★★★★★
😊 Confidence & Fluency Speaks without long pauses; doesn't freeze during challenges; recovers naturally from mistakes ★★★★★
