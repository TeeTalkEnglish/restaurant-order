👨‍🏫 Teacher Guide: Fine Dining at NOMAD Melbourne

Advanced hospitality roleplay scenarios for high-level students

📋 Program Overview

NOMAD Melbourne provides advanced roleplay scenarios designed to teach sophisticated hospitality skills. Students encounter diverse guests from VIP couples to challenging service situations, mastering:

  • VIP guest recognition and personalized service
  • Wine pairing expertise and recommendations
  • Multi-course service management and timing
  • Sophisticated language and etiquette
  • Problem-solving with grace and professionalism
  • Special requests and dietary accommodations
  • Time-pressured service and efficiency
  • Severe allergy management and cross-contamination prevention
  • Complaint recovery and service failure resolution
  • Values-aligned guest communication

🎯 Learning Outcomes

After completing this module, students will be able to:

1. Welcome VIP Guests Appropriately

Recognize important guests and acknowledge special occasions with warmth and professionalism

2. Recommend Wine Pairings

Suggest wines confidently based on food selections and guest preferences

3. Guide Multi-Course Service

Explain courses, manage timing, and describe dishes with sophistication

4. Handle Special Requests & Allergies

Manage allergies, dietary restrictions, cross-contamination prevention, and modifications with expertise and care

5. Resolve Problems Professionally

Address complaints with empathy, involve management, offer solutions, and turn service failures into loyalty

6. Use Sophisticated Language

Employ fine dining vocabulary and formal register appropriately across diverse guest types

7. Manage Time Pressure Efficiently

Coordinate with kitchen, streamline orders, and deliver quality service under tight deadlines

8. Adapt to Different Guest Needs

Read situations quickly and adjust service style to VIP guests, business diners, experts, activists, and difficult customers

👥 Guest Personas

Students will encounter eight distinct scenarios, from VIP to challenging service situations:

1. Mr. & Mrs. Thornton – Anniversary Celebration

Type: VIP Couple celebrating special occasion

Key Focus: Warmth, wine pairing, sophistication, personal attention

Teaching Point: VIP service is about making guests feel special and valued

2. James Chen – Business Executive

Type: Professional hosting important client meeting

Key Focus: Discretion, timing flexibility, invisible service

Teaching Point: Business diners need attentive but unobtrusive service

Skills Tested: Professional greeting, timing management, accommodation requests

3. Sophia & Marcus – Food Critics

Type: Experienced diners with expertise

Key Focus: Technical knowledge, storytelling, detailed descriptions

Teaching Point: Sophisticated guests appreciate detailed explanations and expertise

Skills Tested: Menu knowledge, wine expertise, handling criticism

4. Rachel & David – Difficult Guests

Type: Discerning guests with high expectations and skepticism

Key Focus: Confidence, problem-solving, turning skeptics into admirers

Teaching Point: Excellence under pressure wins over even difficult guests

Skills Tested: Confidence, dietary accommodation, complaint resolution

5. Priya Kapoor – Vegan Activist

Type: Values-driven guest with ethical dining concerns

Key Focus: Sustainability, plant-based excellence, values alignment

Teaching Point: Activists become powerful advocates when they see authentic commitment

Skills Tested: Values respect, sustainable sourcing knowledge, non-defensive responses

6. Michael Torres – Culinary Student

Type: Aspiring chef with serious technique questions

Key Focus: Technical knowledge, mentorship, supporting next generation

Teaching Point: Expert diners don't want hand-holding; they want knowledge sharing

Skills Tested: Technical expertise, teaching ability, Chef communication

7. Alex & Jordan – Rush Hour Couple ⏰

Type: Time-pressured diners with theater tickets

Key Focus: Efficiency, speed without sacrificing quality, precise timing

Teaching Point: Fast service requires coordination with kitchen and clear communication

Skills Tested: Timeline management, streamlined ordering, kitchen coordination, quick problem-solving

8. Casey & Sam – Dietary Restrictions Group ⚠️

Type: Careful diners with severe allergies and cross-contamination concerns

Key Focus: Detailed protocols, cross-contamination prevention, transparency

Teaching Point: Allergy management builds trust and loyalty when done meticulously

Skills Tested: Allergy protocol knowledge, detailed menu review, Chef coordination, empathy

9. Morgan & Taylor – Disappointed Diner 😕

Type: Guests who received substandard food that didn't meet expectations

Key Focus: Complaint recovery, empathy, service recovery, turning problems into loyalty

Teaching Point: How you recover from mistakes defines your restaurant's reputation

Skills Tested: Complaint listening, management escalation, replacement execution, follow-up care

📚 Fine Dining Vocabulary & Phrases

VIP Greeting Phrases

  • "Welcome to NOMAD. How lovely to see you this evening."
  • "I understand this is a special occasion – you both look absolutely magnificent."
  • "Might I suggest our private corner table for more intimate conversation?"
  • "We're honored to have you join us this evening."

Wine Pairing Vocabulary

  • Minerality, acidity, tannins, body (light/full), notes, bouquet
  • "This Cabernet pairs beautifully with our premium beef."
  • "Notice the interplay between the wine's acidity and the fish's richness."
  • "This Chardonnay is elegant and refined, perfect for the seafood course."

Course Presentation Phrases

  • "May I present your first course?"
  • "This is pan-seared diver scallop, sourced fresh this morning from Tasmania."
  • "Notice the delicate edible flowers and the velvety saffron sauce."
  • "The scallop is seared to a perfect golden exterior while maintaining tender sweetness inside."

Handling Special Requests & Allergies

  • "We take allergies extremely seriously – never just 'being careful.'"
  • "Let me explain our cross-contamination protocols: separate prep area, dedicated equipment, Chef oversight."
  • "Your questions are exactly right – documenting these shows you're taking your safety seriously."
  • "We absolutely can work with that – our Chef specializes in accommodating dietary needs."
  • "I'll ensure our Chef is informed immediately."
  • "This is your evening, and we're here to make it exceptional."

Time-Pressured Service Phrases

  • "I see you're on a schedule. Let me be direct about timing so we can work together."
  • "Our appetizer-to-table is 12 minutes, mains are 18-20 minutes. We can absolutely make your timeline."
  • "Perfect strategy – I'll coordinate with our kitchen so everything comes out on schedule."
  • "You're staying right on track – we'll have you out by [specific time]."

Complaint Resolution Phrases

  • "I sincerely apologize – you're absolutely right, and this isn't acceptable."
  • "Your feedback actually helps us improve. Thank you for being honest."
  • "Let me take this back immediately and have our Chef prepare a fresh [dish] cooked perfectly."
  • "I'm going to bring our manager out – they should hear this directly from you."
  • "Here's your replacement – our Chef prepared this personally with extra care."
  • "Thank you for your grace in how you handled that. You've earned our gratitude."

Allergy Safety Communication

  • "Gluten-free? We have a dedicated prep station with separate cutting boards and fryer."
  • "Our Chef will personally handle your plate preparation from start to finish."
  • "Before your plate leaves the kitchen, the Chef confirms it personally."
  • "Your meal was prepared in our dedicated [allergen] station by our Chef personally."

💡 Teaching Tips

Before Starting

  • Ensure students understand the difference between casual and fine dining service
  • Review the menu together and discuss wine pairings
  • Discuss what makes a guest "VIP" and the importance of recognition
  • Practice sophisticated language and formal register

During the Roleplay

  • Encourage students to take risks and try sophisticated language
  • If a guest scenario appears, use it as a teaching moment
  • Ask students to explain the 'why' behind their choices (why that wine, why that timing)
  • Have students practice scenario multiple times with different guests

After the Roleplay

  • Discuss what went well and what could be improved
  • Ask: "How did you make this guest feel special?"
  • Review wine pairing choices – were they appropriate?
  • Discuss alternatives for difficult situations
  • Encourage reflection on professional hospitality

📊 Assessment Rubric

Use this rubric to evaluate student performance across different scenarios:

Professional Greeting (5 points)

  • Recognizes guest type, occasion, and time constraints
  • Offers personalized welcome appropriate to situation
  • Sets professional, welcoming tone immediately

Wine Selection (5 points)

  • Makes appropriate recommendation based on guest needs
  • Explains pairing logic with confidence
  • Shows flexibility for different guest types

Menu Guidance (5 points)

  • Describes dishes with detail and sophistication
  • Adapts explanations to guest knowledge level
  • Answers questions knowledgeably and completely

Allergy & Special Requests (5 points)

  • Takes allergies extremely seriously – never minimizes concerns
  • Explains cross-contamination protocols specifically
  • Involves Chef directly and shows confidence in solutions
  • Provides detailed menu reviews for dietary restrictions

Time Management & Efficiency (5 points)

  • Acknowledges time constraints and communicates realistic timelines
  • Streamlines ordering process when needed
  • Coordinates kitchen timing to meet guest deadlines

Complaint Resolution (5 points)

  • Listens to complaints without defensiveness
  • Apologizes sincerely and takes responsibility
  • Escalates to management appropriately
  • Executes replacement with exceptional care

Language & Professionalism (5 points)

  • Uses appropriate formal register and vocabulary
  • Maintains professional composure under pressure
  • Adapts communication style to different guest types

Total: 35 points

🚀 Extension Activities

  • Wine Tasting: Research Australian wines and practice descriptions
  • Menu Design: Have students create their own fine dining menu with descriptions
  • Time Management Training: Practice streamlining orders under pressure; coordinate timing with actual kitchen
  • Allergy Protocol Simulation: Role-play different allergen scenarios; practice cross-contamination questions
  • Complaint Handling Workshop: Discuss various dining failures and practice service recovery responses
  • Guest Interaction Practice: Pair students and have one act as guest, one as waiter – rotate through different scenarios
  • Video Analysis: Watch fine dining service videos and critique techniques, noting what works and what doesn't
  • Research Projects: Research fine dining etiquette, allergy protocols, emergency procedures
  • Role Reversal: Have students play difficult guests to understand the server's perspective when handling complaints
  • Menu Allergy Training: Create detailed charts showing which menu items contain common allergens
